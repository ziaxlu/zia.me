moodboard

As the first step in creating my website, I started by crafting a moodboard to define the foundation of my site design—minimalistic, yet infused with spontaneity through doodles and organic shapes.

I aimed to reflect my own personality, which is initially reserved but gradually becomes louder and more outgoing as I connect with others. To achieve this, I envisioned a simple black & white palette that transforms into a burst of color as visitors interact with different elements, encouraging them to explore deeper into the site.

In making the website more personal, I decided to create a custom font of my own handwriting, which now make the page headers and titles to add a touch of authenticity and uniqueness. For the body text, I opted for a clean sans-serif font, ensuring readability while maintaining the simplicity of the overall layout.

low fidelity wireframe

With my design style and colors decided, my next step was to plan the pages and structure of my website. I decided to include pages for an introduction, past experience, projects, and to document my website creation process, as well as a landing page to tie it all together.

I then created a lo-fi wireframe to roughly lay out the structure of each page—where the images and text should be—along with some notes on my vision for the elements.

custom font

Before I could proceed in filling in the lo-fi wireframe with images and text that would appear in the final website, I needed to create my custom handwriting font.

To do this, I filled in a template of letters and characters with my neatest handwriting. To make a bold version, I simply added a stroke to the outside of the regular version. Once all filled out, I downloaded my new font and put it to use in my hi-fi wireframe.

high fidelity wireframe

With my custom handwriting font ready, I began preparing the various images, doodles, and text that make up each page of my website. Keeping the color palette and design language in mind, I then assembled the elements together, creating a high fidelity that would closely resemble the look of my final site.

Creating a high fidelity wireframe before entering the coding stage allowed me to play around with different colors and styles without having to do so through code, which saved a lot of time (and sanity).

coding

In order to bring my designs to life, I learned of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Using my newfound knowledge on the basics of web development, I managed to slowly but surely build the website I envisioned from scratch.
